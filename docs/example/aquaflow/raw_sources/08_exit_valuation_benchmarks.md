# Exit Strategies and Valuation Benchmarks — Private Equity

## Overview

Exit strategy planning begins at LBO entry and is updated continuously through the hold period. A PE sponsor's ability to achieve an attractive exit is the ultimate test of investment thesis execution. The three primary exit paths — strategic sale, sponsor-to-sponsor (S2S) secondary, and IPO — each carry different valuation dynamics, process timelines, and requirements.

Exit valuation benchmarks serve as the foundation for LBO returns modeling: the exit EBITDA multiplied by the projected exit multiple determines the enterprise value from which the equity residual is calculated.

## Exit Path 1: Strategic Sale

A strategic buyer (corporate acquirer) is typically the highest-value exit path for mid-market PE companies. Strategic buyers can pay premium multiples because they capture synergies — cost savings from consolidating overlapping functions, revenue synergies from cross-selling, or strategic value from acquiring a technology or market position.

**Typical strategic buyer rationale:**
- Geographic expansion into a new market (no greenfield cost)
- Technology acquisition (faster than organic development)
- Eliminating a competitor or blocking a competitor from acquiring the asset

**Strategic sale multiple premiums:**
Strategics typically pay 1–2x EBITDA turns above financial buyer (PE) prices, driven by synergy capture. A company valued by a PE buyer at 8.5x EBITDA might command 9.5–10.5x from a strategic.

**Process:**
Investment banker runs a controlled auction (6–8 weeks preliminary; 4–6 weeks final round); management presentations; final bids; exclusivity; due diligence and signing; regulatory approval; close. Total timeline typically 5–8 months.

**Requirements for strategic readiness:**
- Clean financial reporting with GAAP-compliant statements (3 years audited)
- No material unresolved litigation or regulatory exposure
- Documentation of customer relationships and contract backlog
- Clean IP ownership (no co-ownership disputes, assignments complete)
- Management team willing to remain through transition (often 1–2 year retention agreements)

## Exit Path 2: Sponsor-to-Sponsor (S2S) Secondary

A secondary buyout occurs when one PE firm sells to another PE firm. S2S secondaries have become increasingly common as PE assets under management have grown: approximately 40% of PE exits by volume are now S2S.

**When S2S makes sense:**
- Strategic buyers are not active acquirers in the sector at time of exit
- Public market IPO window is unfavorable
- Asset has more value creation runway (the incoming sponsor sees an angle the seller has not fully executed)
- Speed and certainty of execution favored over maximum price

**S2S valuation:**
S2S multiples are generally below strategic premiums but above public market comparables. Financial buyers model their own levered returns and cannot pay for synergies they do not have. However, competitive secondary auctions can drive S2S multiples to within 0.5–1.0x of strategic bids.

**Key S2S considerations:**
- The incoming sponsor will conduct full LBO DD, including a new QoE — expect 8–12 weeks of process
- Management rollover expectations: incoming sponsor typically expects 5–30% management rollover
- Existing debt must be refinanced or assumed; credit market conditions matter

## Exit Path 3: IPO

An initial public offering (IPO) converts PE equity into publicly traded shares. IPOs can maximize value in favorable market windows, but carry significant execution risk, preparation costs, and timing uncertainty.

**Conditions for IPO viability:**
- Revenue typically >$300–500M (sub-$300M companies rarely sustain investor interest)
- EBITDA margins ≥ 20% or growth rate ≥ 25% revenue CAGR
- Clean corporate governance (independent board, audit committee, public reporting controls)
- Favorable sector market sentiment and IPO window
- Compelling equity story with differentiated positioning vs. public comps

**IPO preparation timeline:**
12–18 months pre-IPO: governance improvements, financial restatements (if needed), auditor relationship.
6–12 months pre-IPO: S-1 filing preparation, underwriter selection, PCAOB-compliant audit.
IPO process: 3–6 months from S-1 filing to closing.

**Lock-up and secondary sales:**
PE sponsors are locked up (cannot sell shares) for 180 days post-IPO. Full liquidity requires a 12–24 month secondary process. This extended liquidity timeline affects IRR calculations.

## Exit Multiple Assumptions in LBO Modeling

Convention: LBO models assume **entry multiple ≥ exit multiple** (conservative base case) to avoid circularity bias. A deal entering at 8.5x should model a 7.5–8.0x exit in the base case; upside case may use entry multiple or slight expansion.

**Multiple contraction sensitivity:**
If entry is at 8.5x and exit contracts to 7.5x on the same EBITDA base, the 1.0x turn reduction costs approximately $75M in enterprise value at a $75M EBITDA base — reducing equity proceeds proportionally.

## Return Attribution

LBO returns decompose into three drivers:

| Driver | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| EBITDA Growth | Organic revenue growth + margin expansion from operations |
| Multiple Expansion | Exit multiple vs. entry multiple (positive if exit > entry) |
| Debt Paydown | Reduction of net debt increases equity residual |

A healthy LBO return attribution has EBITDA growth as the primary driver (60–70%). Excessive reliance on leverage (debt paydown) or multiple expansion is a sign of financial engineering rather than value creation.

## AquaFlow Exit Model — Worked Example

The following illustrates a probability-weighted exit analysis for AquaFlow Systems (entry EV $635M / $74.8M LTM EBITDA / 8.5x entry multiple; sponsor equity check $261M; Net Debt $374M at entry).

### Exit Path Probability Weights

| Exit Path | Probability | Multiple Range | Base Case |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| S2S Secondary | 50% | 8.5–10.0x | 9.0x |
| Strategic Sale | 35% | 10.0–11.5x | 10.5x |
| IPO | 15% | 9.0–13.0x | 11.0x |

**S2S secondary (50%):** Most probable given AquaFlow's $312.4M revenue — sub-platform scale positions it as a bolt-on rather than a standalone platform IPO candidate. An incoming sponsor acquires for further growth, PFAS platform build-out, or regional consolidation. Multiple range 8.5–10.0x reflects financial buyer pricing without strategic synergies.

**Strategic sale (35%):** Xylem, Veolia Water Technologies, and Hydra Solutions Corp. (est. $520M revenue) represent the primary buyer universe. Strategics pay 1–2x EBITDA premium over financial buyers to capture synergies. Multiple range 10.0–11.5x; requires clean PFAS indemnity resolution and B+/A- ESG sub-score for sustainability-mandate buyers.

**IPO (15%):** Borderline eligibility at $312M revenue (public market investor preference is >$300–500M). Requires EBITDA margins ≥20% (AquaFlow at 23.9% qualifies), favorable sector IPO window, and improvement in social sub-score from B- to A- for institutional LP screening. Multiple range 9.0–13.0x depending on market conditions.

### Year-4 Base-Case Exit (S2S at 9.0x)

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Year-4 Adjusted EBITDA | $117M |
| Exit Multiple | 9.0x |
| Exit Enterprise Value | $1,053M |
| Remaining Net Debt at Exit | $215M |
| Equity Value at Exit | $838M |
| Sponsor Equity Check (entry) | ~$261M |
| MOIC | ~3.2x |
| IRR (4-year hold) | ~33% |

**Year-4 EBITDA derivation:** $74.8M base × ~8% revenue CAGR to ~$425M revenue, with margin expansion from 23.9% to ~27.5% (consistent with management's projected 26–28% by 2027), yields approximately $117M EBITDA.

**Debt paydown:** From $374M net debt at entry to $215M at exit — a $159M reduction driven by 1% TLB amortization ($3.2M/year), plus excess cash sweep of 50–75% of FCF. By year 4, Net Debt/EBITDA contracts from 5.0x entry to approximately 1.8x, substantially expanding the equity cushion.

**MOIC:** $838M exit equity / $261M equity check ≈ 3.2x MOIC. At 4-year hold: (3.2)^(1/4) – 1 ≈ 33% IRR.

### Return Attribution

| Driver | Contribution |
|--------|-------------|
| EBITDA Growth | ~64% |
| Debt Paydown | ~27% |
| Multiple Expansion | ~0% |
| **Total** | **~91%** (remainder = hold-period effects) |

EBITDA growth contribution: ($117M – $74.8M) × 9.0x = $379.8M incremental EV / $577M total equity gain ≈ 64%. Debt paydown contribution: $159M / $577M ≈ 27%. Multiple expansion: 0% (conservative convention: model exit = entry multiple of 9.0x; actual entry was 8.5x, so this is slightly conservative). A healthy LBO return attribution has EBITDA growth as the primary driver (60–70%).

### Three Pre-Exit Risk Factors

1. **DMWA re-bid (December 31, 2024):** The $19.4M Denver Metro Water Authority contract (6.2% of LTM revenue) is subject to competitive re-bid. Loss would compress EBITDA by ~$4–5M directly (service contract margin ~65%) and raise customer concentration for remaining top-10 customers — a binary event in years 1–2 that must be resolved before exit.

2. **B- social ESG sub-score:** Turnover 18.4%, LTIR 1.8 vs. sector 1.6, DEI gap at VP+. Must improve to B+ by year 3–4 to access ESG-mandate strategic buyers and clear institutional LP screens for IPO. Failure to remediate compresses strategic exit multiple by 0.5–1.0x.

3. **59% recurring revenue threshold:** AquaFlow's 59% recurring revenue is just below the >60% threshold that supports upper-end multiples. If QoE reclassifies any service revenue as transactional, the company falls further below 60%, compressing multiple support. Maintaining and growing the recurring base is the primary value-creation lever.

### Downside Scenario — Combined Risk Impact

If all three pre-exit risks materialize simultaneously (DMWA lost, ESG score remains B-, recurring revenue reclassified below 60%), the combined EV impact is approximately **$187M** (2.5x multiple turn × $74.8M EBITDA). This scenario reduces exit equity to ~$651M and MOIC to ~2.5x — below the typical ≥3.0x LP return threshold.

## Exit Readiness Checklist

12 months before targeted exit:
- Audited financial statements clean for 3+ years
- Material litigation resolved or reserved
- Management team stable and incentivized (MIP vesting aligned)
- Key customer contracts renewed; no material expirations in next 12 months
- IP ownership confirmed clean; no pending validity challenges
- ESG improvement program documented with third-party assurance
- Board composition upgraded for public-company readiness (if IPO considered)
- CIM-ready financial model with LTM and 3-year projections
- Investment banker selection and M&A process strategy defined
